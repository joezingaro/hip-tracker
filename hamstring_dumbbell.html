Heavy enough to feel it — not a balance exercise
Dumbbell in right hand for left leg. Hinge from the left hip — keep it level, don't rotate open. Feel the left hamstring and glute loading. Soft left knee throughout. This is your highest-value exercise given the hamstring graft and left glute med history.
Left-side focus: always do left leg first. Right side will be stronger — that's expected. The asymmetry narrowing over months is your progress marker.
Group B Pick 1 — hinge pattern
Don't do both the RDL and Good Morning in the same session — they're too similar.
Romanian deadlift (RDL)
3–4 × 10–15 · bilateral
HamstringsGlutesBeginner–Intermediate
▼
Sets & Reps
3–4 sets × 10–15 reps
Hold
Both hands, one end of dumbbell
Hinge at hips pushing them back, flat back, soft knees. Lower until deep hamstring stretch (mid-shin), then drive hips forward to stand. Keep the dumbbell close to your legs throughout.
Watch for: the right side dominating. If one hip is hinging more than the other, slow down and feel for symmetry.
Good morning
3 × 10–12 · light-moderate
HamstringsGlutesIntermediate
▼
Sets & Reps
3 sets × 10–12 reps
Hold
Both hands behind head or hugged to chest
Braced core, slight knee bend. Hinge at hips until torso nearly parallel to floor. Keep back completely flat — do not round. Feel the hamstring stretch, drive hips forward to stand. Start light — this loads the lower back more than an RDL.
Easier version: reduce range of motion — hinge only to 45° until you're comfortable with the movement pattern.
Group C Pick 1 — squat or lunge pattern
Don't do both reverse lunge and sumo squat in the same session.
Reverse lunge
3 × 10–12 per leg · goblet hold
GlutesHamstringsBeginner–Intermediate
▼
Sets & Reps
3 sets × 10–12 per leg
Hold
Goblet position at chest
Step back, lower back knee toward floor. Keep front shin vertical and torso upright. Drive through front heel to return. Loads glutes and hamstrings of the front leg. Gentler on the knees than forward lunges.
Left-side watch: the left knee will tend to cave in on the left-leg-forward version. Focus on keeping the left knee tracking over the second toe throughout.
Sumo squat

GlutesInner hamstringsBeginner

Sets & Reps
3–4 sets × 12–15 reps
Hold
Both hands, hang vertically between legs
Feet wider than shoulder-width, toes out 30–45°. Squat to parallel, chest up, knees tracking over toes. Drive through heels, squeeze glutes at the top.
Deeper range: stand on two weight plates or books to allow the dumbbell to drop lower, increasing the range of motion through the glutes.
Group D Pick 1 — finisher (optional but recommended)
Hip thrust / glute bridge
3–4 × 15–20 · dumbbell across hips
GlutesHamstringsBeginner
▼
Sets & Reps
3–4 sets × 15–20 reps
Hold
Across hips, both hands stabilising
Lie on back, knees bent, dumbbell across hip crease. Drive through heels until shoulders to knees form a straight line. Squeeze glutes hard at top for 1–2 seconds, lower slowly. For a hip thrust, rest upper back on a couch or bench for greater range.
Single-leg version: extend one leg and do single-leg glute bridges — left side will feel significantly weaker. This is a useful asymmetry check and a progression target.
Always warm up with 5–10 min of light cardio and dynamic stretches. Do the pre-session activation exercises before this — banded side steps, single-leg stance, single-leg bridge.
