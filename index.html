Pre-session activation (always)
Banded side steps
20 steps each direction · band above knees
▼
Band just above knees. Walk sideways keeping toes forward and hips level. Feel it deep in the outer left hip — not the front of the thigh. If you feel it in the TFL (front/outer hip), slow down and widen your stance slightly.
Single-leg stance hold
Left leg only · 3 × 20 sec
▼
Stand on left leg. Keep the right hip from dropping. If it drops, that's the weakness showing. Slight knee bend. Engage the outer left hip actively — don't just balance passively.
Single-leg glute bridge
Left leg only · 2 × 15 reps
▼
Lie on back, left foot flat, right leg extended. Drive through left heel to lift hips. Squeeze left glute hard at the top for 1 second. Don't let the right hip sag. Lower slowly — 3 counts down.
Add to your lift
Single-leg RDL
Left leg · 3 × 10 · go heavy enough to feel it
▼
Dumbbell in right hand. Hinge from the left hip — keep it level, don't rotate open. Feel the left hamstring and glute loading. Soft left knee throughout. This is your highest-value corrective given the graft history.
Step-down
Left leg · 2 × 10 slow · your weekly quality check
▼
Stand on a step on the left leg. Slowly lower the right foot toward the floor — don't touch it — then return. Watch for: knee caving in, hip dropping right, trunk shifting. Note whether it feels better than last week.
Copenhagen adductor hold
Left side · 3 × 20–30 sec
▼
Lie on your right side, left foot resting on a bench at hip height. Lift hips off the floor using the left inner thigh — hold straight. Adductors and glute med work as a force couple. Start shorter if it's hard.

Fatigue-state check
Last 20% of your run
When tired — this is when the left hip drops. Check in: is the right hip dropping? Is the trunk drifting right? Noticing it starts correcting it. Do the banded steps immediately after your run — the fatigued state is when rewiring happens fastest.
Cycling focus

Pre-ride prime
Do activation before you clip in
Do all 3 activation exercises before getting on the bike. The bilateral pedal stroke lets the right side dominate silently. Priming first means the left glute med shows up from stroke one. Saddle check: if the left hip rocks down at the bottom of the stroke, saddle may be too high.

Pushup progression
3 sets · stop 1–2 reps before failure
▼
You're at ~15 clean reps. Don't grind out 20 standard pushups every day — that plateaus fast. Rotate variations week to week: standard → close grip → archer (one arm reaches wide, load shifts to working side) → deficit (hands on dumbbells for deeper range). Stop 1–2 reps before form breaks. Quality over volume.
Band pull-apart
3 × 15 · light-medium band
▼
Hold band at shoulder height, arms straight. Pull apart until band touches chest — squeeze shoulder blades together at the end, don't just pull with arms. Directly counteracts the right-shoulder-forward posture from your thoracic rotation asymmetry. Every push needs a pull — this is it on 5 min days.
Pike pushup
2 × 8–10 · shoulder strength
▼
Hips high, body in an inverted V. Bend elbows to lower the top of your head toward the floor, press back up. Targets the shoulders overhead with no equipment. As this gets easy, elevate feet on a chair to increase the angle.
15 min extras · WFH days only
Single-arm dumbbell row
Left side first · 3 × 10–12 each
▼
Always do left side first. Brace with left hand and knee on a bench. Row dumbbell to hip, lead with elbow, squeeze left shoulder blade in at the top. Directly addresses left shoulder blade instability — the upper body mirror of what we're doing with the left glute med.
Dumbbell shoulder press
3 × 10 · seated or standing
▼
Press both dumbbells overhead. Watch for the right shoulder shrugging or elbow flaring more than the left — that's the asymmetry showing. If right dominates, drop to single-arm and do left separately. Keep ribs down, don't arch the lower back.
Dead hang
2 × 20–30 sec · if bar accessible
▼
Hang from pull-up bar — let the shoulders fully decompress, don't engage anything. Best single thing for shoulder health and thoracic decompression. Given your thoracic rotation pattern, passive hanging lets the spine and shoulder girdle find neutral. Skip only if bar isn't convenient.

What kind of break?
Every 60–90 min. Undoes exactly what the last hour of sitting has reinforced. 90 seconds total.
Kneeling hip flexor stretch
Left knee down · 30–45 sec · with glute squeeze
▼
Left knee on the floor, right foot forward. Before leaning in — squeeze the left glute actively first. This locks the pelvis and forces the actual hip flexor to lengthen rather than the pelvis cheating. Then slowly shift weight forward until you feel a deep stretch in the front of the left hip. Hold. This is your single most important desk break given the pattern.
Standing left glute med activation
Left leg · 2 × 10 sec hold
▼
Stand on left leg. Actively engage the outer left hip — keep the right hip from dropping. 10 second hold. The glute med has been completely switched off for the last hour. This fires it back up before you go back to sitting. Two holds is enough — quality over quantity here.
Thoracic rotation left
Standing · 8 slow reps
▼
Stand, arms crossed over chest. Rotate upper body slowly to the left — left is your restricted direction. Hold 2 seconds at end range. Sitting at a desk reinforces rightward thoracic rotation all day. Eight reps left takes 30 seconds and directly counters that.

Tripod foot check
Left foot · 5 seconds
▼
Feel your left foot on the floor. Three points of contact — heel, base of little toe, base of big toe — all pressing equally. Sitting tends to roll the left foot inward onto the inner edge. Resetting the tripod contact wakes up the lateral chain even while seated.
Rib position reset
Seated · 5 seconds
▼
Sitting tends to flare the left ribs and compress the right. Take a deep breath and on the exhale, gently draw the left ribs down and in. Feel the left side of your trunk lengthen slightly. This directly addresses the rib asymmetry from your thoracic rotation pattern. Takes five seconds seated at your desk.
Shoulder level check
Seated · 5 seconds
▼
Notice where your right shoulder is sitting. It's almost certainly higher and more forward than the left. Gently roll it back and down — don't force it, just release the held tension in the right upper trap. This is passive release, not an exercise. You're just becoming aware of a pattern that's happening unconsciously all day.
Seated left glute squeeze
3 × 5 sec isometric
▼
Sitting in your chair — consciously contract the left glute as hard as you can for 5 seconds, then release. Sounds almost too simple. But after an hour of sitting with the glute completely inhibited, this isometric fires the muscle and maintains some neuromuscular connection. Three squeezes, done while reading an email. No one will ever know.

Your correction pathway
← You are here
Phase 1
Activation
Weeks 1–10 · every session
Building the mind-muscle connection on the left side. The goal isn't strength yet — it's felt activation. You're teaching the nervous system to recruit the left glute med instead of defaulting to the TFL and right-side compensation. Keep all your normal training. Add the 3 activation exercises as a pre-session warm-up every time.
Phase 2
Loaded strength
Weeks 6–20 · overlaps with Phase 1
Real load on the left side. Single-leg RDLs, Copenhagen holds, step-downs with control. The step-down test is your weekly benchmark — measurably less knee cave and hip drop over this period. Bilateral deadlifts stay. You're adding single-leg work alongside them, not replacing.
Phase 3
Sport integration
Months 5–12
The left glute med needs to fire correctly at pace, under fatigue, in cuts and sprints — not just in the gym. Fatigue-state checks during runs and second-half soccer awareness are your tools. By month 9–12 the compensation pattern should be significantly reduced even late in hard sessions.
Key milestones
Weeks 6–10
Left side activation clearly felt during exercises
Months 3–4
Step-down test visibly more symmetric left vs right
Month 5–6
Left lateral hip tightness noticeably reduced
Month 8–12
Pattern holds under fatigue — soccer 2nd half, long run back-half
Remember
Keep the deadlifts. Keep the bike. Keep the soccer.

The program wraps around your training. 20 minutes of targeted work added to sessions you're already doing is enough. A program you actually do for 12 months beats a perfect program you abandon at week 6.

One physio session in month 1–2 to confirm your activation is hitting the glute med and not the TFL will save months of working on the wrong thing.
