Pre-session activation (always)
Banded side steps
20 steps each direction · band above knees
▼
Band just above knees. Walk sideways keeping toes forward and hips level. Feel it deep in the outer left hip — not the front of the thigh. If you feel it in the TFL (front/outer hip), slow down and widen your stance slightly.
Single-leg stance hold
Left leg only · 3 × 20 sec
▼
Stand on left leg. Keep the right hip from dropping. If it drops, that's the weakness showing. Slight knee bend. Engage the outer left hip actively — don't just balance passively.
Single-leg glute bridge
Left leg only · 2 × 15 reps
▼
Lie on back, left foot flat, right leg extended. Drive through left heel to lift hips. Squeeze left glute hard at the top for 1 second. Don't let the right hip sag. Lower slowly — 3 counts down.
Add to your lift
Single-leg RDL
Left leg · 3 × 10 · go heavy enough to feel it
▼
Dumbbell in right hand. Hinge from the left hip — keep it level, don't rotate open. Feel the left hamstring and glute loading. Soft left knee throughout. This is your highest-value corrective given the graft history.
Step-down
Left leg · 2 × 10 slow · your weekly quality check
▼
Stand on a step on the left leg. Slowly lower the right foot toward the floor — don't touch it — then return. Watch for: knee caving in, hip dropping right, trunk shifting. Note whether it feels better than last week.
Copenhagen adductor hold
Left side · 3 × 20–30 sec
▼
Lie on your right side, left foot resting on a bench at hip height. Lift hips off the floor using the left inner thigh — hold straight. Adductors and glute med work as a force couple. Start shorter if it's hard.
Running focus
👁
Fatigue-state check
Last 20% of your run
When tired — this is when the left hip drops. Check in: is the right hip dropping? Is the trunk drifting right? Noticing it starts correcting it. Do the banded steps immediately after your run — the fatigued state is when rewiring happens fastest.
Cycling focus
🎯
Pre-ride prime
Do activation before you clip in
Do all 3 activation exercises before getting on the bike. The bilateral pedal stroke lets the right side dominate silently. Priming first means the left glute med shows up from stroke one. Saddle check: if the left hip rocks down at the bottom of the stroke, saddle may be too high.
Soccer focus
⚡
Late-game awareness
2nd half · fatigue state
Left knee diving in on cuts, left hip feeling heavy or less explosive — this is the compensation pattern showing. Normal given the history. The goal over months is that this gap narrows. Note any asymmetry you feel today when you log.

Your correction pathway
← You are here
Phase 1
Activation
Weeks 1–10 · every session
Building the mind-muscle connection on the left side. The goal isn't strength yet — it's felt activation. You're teaching the nervous system to recruit the left glute med instead of defaulting to the TFL and right-side compensation. Keep all your normal training. Add the 3 activation exercises as a pre-session warm-up every time.
Phase 2
Loaded strength
Weeks 6–20 · overlaps with Phase 1
Real load on the left side. Single-leg RDLs, Copenhagen holds, step-downs with control. The step-down test is your weekly benchmark — measurably less knee cave and hip drop over this period. Bilateral deadlifts stay. You're adding single-leg work alongside them, not replacing.
Phase 3
Sport integration
Months 5–12
The left glute med needs to fire correctly at pace, under fatigue, in cuts and sprints — not just in the gym. Fatigue-state checks during runs and second-half soccer awareness are your tools. By month 9–12 the compensation pattern should be significantly reduced even late in hard sessions.
Key milestones
Weeks 6–10
Left side activation clearly felt during exercises
Months 3–4
Step-down test visibly more symmetric left vs right
Month 5–6
Left lateral hip tightness noticeably reduced
Month 8–12
Pattern holds under fatigue — soccer 2nd half, long run back-half
Remember
Keep the deadlifts. Keep the bike. Keep the soccer.

The program wraps around your training. 20 minutes of targeted work added to sessions you're already doing is enough. A program you actually do for 12 months beats a perfect program you abandon at week 6.

One physio session in month 1–2 to confirm your activation is hitting the glute med and not the TFL will save months of working on the wrong thing.
